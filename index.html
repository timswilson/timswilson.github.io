On coaching

I've been coaching youth soccer for several seasons now. My philosophy is simple: kids should leave practice a little more confident than they arrived. Technique matters, but so does effort, curiosity, and the ability to laugh when you shank one over the crossbar. I try to run practices that feel more like games than drills, and I measure success by whether players want to come back the next week.

01

Play with joy

If it stops being fun, we've lost the plot. Competitive fire is welcome; grim faces are not.

02

Effort over outcome

We celebrate hustle, smart decisions, and bravery on the ball — scoreline second.

03

Every player matters

Equal minutes, real coaching, real feedback. The bench is not a waiting room.

04

Teach the game

I want players to read the field, not just follow instructions. Questions beat commands.
